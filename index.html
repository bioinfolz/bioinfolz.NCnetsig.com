CisASE

CisASE is a likelihood based method for ASE detection at multiple levels(SNV,exon and gene). It takes matched DNA sequencing data as reference to reduce artifact from technical bias and copy number variation to identify ASE due to putative cis-regulation, and it works almost as well for dataset without DNA sequencing.

Workflow of cisASE

Raw RNA-Seq and DNA-Seq data were processed to pileup at each allele, and then submitted to a likelihood model to calculate the likelihood ratio under two models (LOD), and null simulation of DNA and RNA allelic counts were generated from real data to produce null distribution of LOD, and defined an LOD cutoff respect to a certain significant level. LOD for each measured SNV was compared to the defined LOD threshold to determine if it’s an ASE or not.
 

Reference

Liu Z, Gui TT, Wang Z, Li H, Dong X, Li YX: A likelihood method for detecting putative cis-regulated allele specific expression in RNA sequencing data.
